Focus Areas

Research Interests

Bridging the gap between static, data-driven optimization and the dynamic, structured reasoning characteristic of human scientific discovery.

Autonomous Agentic Architectures & Self-Evolution

Pioneering open-ended agents (SAMKE, SOLAR, CORAL, ALIAS) capable of self-improvement not merely by learning from data, but by exploring the weight space to learn how to adapt — validating the feasibility of self-evolving AI scientists.

Cognitive-Inspired Learning & Knowledge Consolidation

Developing human-pedagogy-inspired training paradigms (LMT², MASA) that simulate the protégé effect — structuring LLM adaptation through learning, mastery and teaching phases — achieving deeper understanding with less data.

Robust Optimization & Structural Decoupling

Identifying and resolving geometric bottlenecks in bi-level meta-learning (FOCAL, DeGAML-LLM, CAO-LLM) through temporal orthogonalization — providing mathematical foundations for stable continual adaptation.
